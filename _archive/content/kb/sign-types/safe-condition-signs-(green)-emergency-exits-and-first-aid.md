## Overview

A guide to green safe condition signs, including emergency exits, first aid stations, and assembly points. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about selecting, installing, and maintaining these critical safety signs.

## Regulatory Framework

These signs are required under:
- Health and Safety (Safety Signs and Signals) Regulations 1996
- BS EN ISO 7010:2020 (current standard)
- Industry-specific regulations (where applicable)

## When These Signs Are Required

According to UK regulations, safety signs must be provided where:

1. **Risks remain** after other control measures
2. **Quick recognition** is essential for safety
3. **Emergency information** must be conveyed
4. **Mandatory actions** are required
5. **Prohibited behaviours** must be prevented

## Sign Design and Standards

### Colour Coding
Each safety sign category uses specific colours with defined meanings:

- **Red** - Prohibition and fire equipment
- **Yellow/Amber** - Warning of hazards
- **Blue** - Mandatory actions required
- **Green** - Safe conditions and emergency information

### Shape Requirements
BS EN ISO 7010:2020 specifies:

- **Circles** - Prohibition and mandatory signs
- **Triangles** - Warning signs
- **Rectangles/Squares** - Information signs

### Pictograms
Standardised symbols ensure universal understanding across:
- Language barriers
- Literacy levels
- Cultural differences
- International workforces

## Installation Requirements

### Height and Positioning
- **Standard mounting height**: 1.5m to 2.0m (eye level)
- **Fire exit signs**: Above door frames or 300mm above floor level
- **Warning signs**: At point of hazard or entry to hazard zone
- **Mandatory signs**: At entry to areas where PPE required

### Visibility Requirements
Signs must be:
- **Adequately lit** or photoluminescent
- **Unobstructed** from normal viewing positions
- **Appropriate size** for viewing distance
- **Clean and legible** at all times

### Size Calculations
Minimum size based on viewing distance:

| Viewing Distance | Minimum Sign Size |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Up to 10m | 150mm x 150mm |
| 10m - 20m | 200mm x 200mm |
| 20m - 30m | 300mm x 300mm |
| 30m - 50m | 400mm x 400mm |

## Fixing Methods

### Screw Fixing
**Best for**: Permanent installations on wood, plaster, metal
- Mark fixing holes
- Drill appropriate pilot holes
- Use wall plugs if required
- Secure with appropriate screws

### Self-Adhesive
**Best for**: Smooth, clean, dry surfaces
- Clean surface thoroughly
- Remove backing paper
- Apply from one edge, avoiding bubbles
- Press firmly, especially edges
- Allow 24 hours before exposure to weather

### Cable Ties
**Best for**: Wire mesh fencing, chain link, barriers
- Use UV-resistant ties outdoors
- Multiple fixing points for large signs
- Check tightness regularly
- Replace degraded ties

### Magnetic Fixings
**Best for**: Temporary signs on steel surfaces
- Clean metal surface
- Strong magnets for outdoor use
- Regular checking required
- Limited to ferrous surfaces

## Maintenance Requirements

### Regular Inspection Schedule
- **Weekly**: Visual check for damage in high-traffic areas
- **Monthly**: Clean signs, check fixings
- **Quarterly**: Detailed inspection, replace damaged signs
- **Annually**: Full audit against risk assessment

### Cleaning Guidelines
1. Use mild detergent and water
2. Soft cloth or sponge only
3. Avoid abrasive cleaners
4. Rinse with clean water
5. Dry thoroughly

### Replacement Indicators
Replace signs when:
- Colours faded (difficult to identify type)
- Physical damage (cracks, chips, bent)
- Text illegible from distance
- Fixings failing
- Signs outdated (pre-ISO 7010)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Wrong Sign Type for Hazard
Using warning signs where prohibition signs are required creates confusion and legal non-compliance.

### 2. Inadequate Size
Signs too small for viewing distance fail to communicate effectively and don't meet regulations.

### 3. Poor Positioning
Signs obscured by doors, equipment, or at wrong heights reduce effectiveness significantly.

### 4. Mixed Standards
Combining old and new symbol systems creates confusion—update all signs together where possible.

### 5. Neglecting Maintenance
Faded or damaged signs give impression of poor safety culture and may not protect legally.
